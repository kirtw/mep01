devlog (and deverr)  is a facility for efficient debugging
    whereby output from a program details internal functionality-
    vars and conditions  - in detail

Init:    S TI("hd")="Log KAcf TOI input "     ;  etc, TI ala HGen, none required, defaults
               Defaults to $PM/wwm/   for simple-pageName links.
          S Q=$$devlog^devIO(fileStem  [,iFol ] )  ; : devlog, open and page-top --add [,iBase]
              I Q'="" D b^dv(Q,"Q,devlog") S devlog=""  ; and proceed log to $P
                 D ^devlog(M,VL)   ala D b^dv(M,VL) ...
          D CFM^devIO(devlog)

Similarly for deverr   $$deverr^devIO(fileStem)

Now HGen to create a page but not paper or scrolling on screen
    available to review in retrospect  or ignore when all is well
    or save occ copies timestamped to much later review,
        to compare, detect subtle changes  line-oriented format enables diff...

var devlog, if undef or null or zero when the arg of USE defaults to $P
    so it does scroll at a minimum.

Init takes place at the start of a program, opening (replacing/overwriting)
    to prevent accumulation of progressive volumes of old files...

Interestingly, the b^dv structure was made to fit ^devlog
    with its M identifying txt message and a list of variables,
        tolerating/documenting UNDEF
  then the M can be easily counted and returnd as an abstract of the whole file.
